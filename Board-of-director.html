Most Rev Dr. Augustine T. Ukwuoma

His Lordship, Most Rev. Dr. Augustine T. Ukwuoma has been on vanguard of God’s vineyard for over 35 years. Born on August 29, 1953 and ordained a Catholic Priest on July 30, 1983, he was ordained as the Bishop of the Catholic Diocese of Orlu on 21st day of June 2008. He holds a Bachelors degree in Philosophy (1979) and Bachelors degree in Theology (1982) from the Bigard Memorial Seminary Ikot Ekpene; a Masters degree from the Loyola Mary Mount University Los Angeles (1979), and a Doctorate degree from the University of Akron, Ohio United States (1994).

Some of the previous positions held prior to his office as the Catholic Bishop of Orlu includes the Administrator Holy Trinity Cathedral Orlu (1992), Associate Pastor St. Ignatius Parish Los Angeles (1994), Priest in Residence Annunciation Parish Akron Ohio (1999), Hospital Chaplain St. Joseph’s Hospital Savannah Georgia (2002), Dean St. Theresa Pastoral Region Uli (2008), Rector St. Theresa Parish Uli (2008), Lecturer, Seat of Wisdom seminary Owerri (2008). He is a member of the Faculty Examination Board, Vicariate (2008), Head of Silver Jubilee Souvenir Committee of Orlu Diocese and President Association of Priests and Religious of Isu Clan (APRIC) He is also a member of Nigerian Institute of Management (NIM), and a Member Financial Reporting Council of Nigeria.

BOARD COMMITTEES AND THEIR MEMBERS

Background Information

The Board of Nwannegadi Microfinance Bank carries out its oversight functions using various committees, making for greater efficiency and deeper attention to specific matters. The Bank has four (4) standing committees and one (1) statutory committee set up in accordance with the Companies and Allied Matters Act (CAMA), 2020. The Board members normally have a minimum of three (3) tenors of 4 years each. The Board leveraged on diverse membership of the committees of the Board to bring the best use of their skills and experience as non-executive directors in particular to enhance the performance of the Bank. The Board had much earlier established the various committees with well-defined terms of reference and charters defining their scope of responsibilities in such a way as to avoid overlap or duplication of functions.

The committees of the Board met quarterly during the period.

BOARD COMMITTEES

The following are the current standing Committees of the Board:

Board establishment committee

Members of the Committee were:

Prof. (Mrs) Dame Theresa A. Udenwa - Chairman

Dame Bibian N. Egeolu - Member

Mr. Christian A. Okwuba - Member

The Committee held meetings every quarter in the year.

Committee's Terms of Reference

To assist the Human Resources Department in the recruitment, discipline and enhancement of the Bank’s personnel quality where the Board so approves.

To be comprised of two or three Board members, one of whom shall be the Managing Director.

 To recommend, where applicable for employment, dismissal or engagement; such candidates as may be presented to it, and the recommendations of the Committee may be accepted by the Board.

To handle matters which deal on promotion, deployment, transfer and disputes issues pertaining to staff, and to communicate its recommendation(s) in writing to the Board.

To ensure regular staff audit, certificates, personnel files, queries, etc, are undertaken by it when necessary, to enhance the performance of the Human Resources Department.

To perform such other duties as may be assigned to it by the Board from time to time.

Board Credit & Risk Committee

Membership of this committee was made up of three (3) Board members: two Non-Executive Directors and one Executive Director. The committee met regularly, but also met at any other time, as the need arose. Members were:

Dame Bibian Nwakaego - Chairman (Non-Executive Director)

Very, Rev. Msgr. John Ukwuoma - Member, (Non-Executive Director)

Dr. Christian A. Okwuba - Managing Director/CEO

The Committee held meetings every quarter in the year under review.

Terms of Reference of the Committee Members were guided by the following terms of reference:

 To ensure that the Bank provides loans and other related financial products/services to micro, small and medium scale enterprises, entrepreneurs, employees and self-employed people which provision(s) shall be made in compliance with the regulations of the CBN, and the Nigerian Deposit Insurance Company (NDIC), the Federal Government of Nigeria (FGN), and also with other banking practices.

To be abreast with Credit Policy Manual of the Bank in order to scrutinize and approve or disapprove applications for loans on the latter’s merits as submitted after appraisal by the Credit Department.

 To ensure the repayment ability of loan applicants, both from the loan application forms and other relevant documentations, and the realistic situation of the customer.

To protect depositors fund by ensuring due diligence in the approval process of loans.

 To unanimously approve loan applications, but, in the events of split options by the members, the vote of the Chairmen shall prevail.

To meet regularly and consider loan applications, in whatever manner of meeting, at least once a month.

To submit, upon recommendations for approval of loan applications, the details to the Chairman of the Board for ratification as stated in the NMFB Credit Policy Manual and observe the limit for lending as found in the Credit Policy Manual. To observe Board/Management credit limit for lending as found in the Credit Policy Manual, which credit limit is subject to change from time to time following Board resolutions.

To perform such other duties as may be assigned to it by the Board from time to time

To observe Board/Management credit limit for lending as found in the Credit Policy Manual, which credit limit is subject to change from time to time following Board resolutions.

Board Audit Committee

The Audit Committee has oversight responsibility for the overall risk assessment of various areas of the Bank’s operations and compliance as well as oversight responsibility regarding audit activities of the Bank. The Chief Risk Officer, the Chief Inspector and the Chief Compliance Officer have access to this committee and make quarterly presentations to it for consideration.

During the period, the Committee was chaired by Very Rev. (Monsignor) Dr. John Ukwuoma Non-Executive Director). The committee’s membership comprised the following:

Very Rev. Msgr Dr. John Ukwuoma - Chairman

Prof. (Mrs) Dame Theresa A. Udenwa - Member - Member

Dame Bibian Nwakaego - Member

The Committee held meetings every quarter in the year under review.

Committee's Terms of Reference remained to:

Ensure that the Audit Policy Manual is in place and regularly updated for the Bank.

Ensure that prudent management of the resources of the Bank is established via policy formulation and advice to the Board.

 Ensure that all measures of control are in place to minimize risks to the Bank and alert the Board from time to time of perceived lapses.

Receive monthly and quarterly reports from the Audit Department and the Risk Management Department, and analyses those reports to enable Board action.

Develop methods and strategic objectives to assess and evaluate risks in order to protect the Bank’s interest.

Develop risk mitigation and control techniques to enhance the performances of the Audit Department and the Risk Management Department.

Give the Board regular information on staff audit, budget performance, and pre and post audit of expenditure.

Perform such other duties as may be assigned to it by the Board from time to time.
